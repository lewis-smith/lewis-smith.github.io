finding a graphic designer for iphone
Aug 17th, 2013

For some reason we thought that finding a graphic designer would be easy…

We’d just post on elance, get loads of replies from talented, experienced and readily available people and then pick one and get some great screens designed.

what we found

As I mentioned in my last post our experience was a little different and we abandoned elance after a frustrating week.

We then moved onto behance and found we had only a slightly better response. We approached one guy whose work we liked, but on speaking with him he didn’t feel like a good fit. We had lots of applicants but a lot didn’t read the brief or provide what we’d asked for.

I then tried looking around on Google. There’s obviously plenty of people to sift through – far too many really -and I went through the first 5 pages of results and contacted a few who I liked. Some weren’t available, some said our budget wasn’t high enough. The rest just couldn’t show us what we wanted to see.

Next I tried LinkedIn. LinkedIn is less cut out for this than I would have expected. Surprisingly few people had their actual work shown online. I found a couple of promising people based solely on their CVs (working with Google, Adobe, Apple, etc) and in the end I did find one person who was our runner up choice.

Finally we got an applicant we liked on behance and after speaking to him on Skype and having a long email conversation with him we’ve decided to go ahead with him. We’re excited to see how it works out. The phrase “how big could this company get if the founders did the right things?” is always in my head when we’re making decisions like this. I’ll let you know how it goes.

what we learned

My biggest take away from this is that we should have allowed a lot more time. Feeling rushed stressed us out and made making a confident decision a lot harder.

Second, right after we’d settled on someone we learned about ooomf. I’d use this next time as it seems to take care of a lot of the hard work for you. I expect the prices would be very high though. (I’d love to hear experiences from either hirers or freelancers on this site).

Third, I wouldn’t use elance again, not for graphic design anyway. I would spend more time combing through behance and dribble looking for people to approach rather than posting a listing and waiting for reactions.

Finally, although at the time I thought we were clear about our requirements I think with hindsight we could have included more detail up front. More importantly I think we could have been more blunt about ignoring people who didn’t send the minimum spec. I’d also set a test like ‘include this phrase at the top of your bid’ to show that candidates have read all the way through the brief.

In short, we’ve learned a lot over the past few weeks and we know what we’d do differently should we need to hire another designer. But, honestly, I really hope we don’t need to go through that process again any time soon.
